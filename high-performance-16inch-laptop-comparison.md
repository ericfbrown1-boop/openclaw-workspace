## Key Considerations Before Purchase

### ✅ Important Decision Factors

#### 1. **Display Technology**
- **OLED** (Alienware, MSI, Lenovo): Perfect blacks, infinite contrast, better for content creation
  - *Downside:* Risk of burn-in with static images, lower peak brightness
- **Mini LED** (ASUS, Razer): Higher peak brightness, no burn-in risk, excellent HDR
  - *Downside:* Blooming around bright objects, not true infinite contrast

#### 2. **Portability vs Performance**
- **Thin & Light** (Razer): 4.71 lbs, 0.69" → Better portability but lower sustained performance (thermal throttling)
- **Standard Gaming** (ASUS, Lenovo, MSI): 5.7-6.0 lbs, 1.0-1.1" → Balanced
- **Workstation** (Alienware): 7.49 lbs, 1.12" → Maximum performance, poor portability

#### 3. **Upgradeability**
- **Fully Upgradeable:** ASUS, Lenovo, Alienware (RAM + storage accessible)
- **Quick Access:** MSI (bottom panel for easy access without full disassembly)
- **Limited:** Razer (soldered RAM, only storage upgradeable, difficult process)

#### 4. **Aesthetics**
- **Professional:** Razer (minimalist black aluminum)
- **Subtle:** Lenovo (understated gaming)
- **Gaming:** ASUS (aggressive ROG styling), MSI (RGB heavy)
- **Bold:** Alienware (distinctive alien aesthetic with RGB)

#### 5. **Warranty & Support**
- **Best:** Alienware/Dell (Premium Support Plus available, excellent reputation)
- **Good:** Lenovo (Legion Ultimate Support available, generally responsive)
- **Average:** ASUS, Razer, MSI (standard manufacturer warranties, variable support experiences)

#### 6. **Real-World Battery Life**
- All models struggle unplugged due to high-power components
- Expect 1-1.5 hours gaming unplugged (regardless of model)
- Light productivity: 4-7 hours (Razer best at 6+ hours)
- Video/browsing: 5-7.5 hours (Razer and Lenovo lead)
- **Recommendation:** Plan to use plugged in for gaming/creative work

#### 7. **Thermal Management & Noise**
- **Quietest:** Razer (but throttles more), Alienware (good balance)
- **Loudest:** MSI (triple fans), ASUS (aggressive fan curves)
- **Coolest:** ASUS, Lenovo (vapor chamber cooling)
- **Hottest:** MSI, Razer (extreme power/thin chassis respectively)
