## Carve-Out Lifecycle

### Pre-Signing (Due Diligence Phase)
1. **Perimeter definition** — Exactly what assets, liabilities, contracts, employees transfer
2. **Standalone cost assessment** — Model the carved-out business as if it operated independently
3. **Stranded cost identification** — Shared services (IT, HR, finance, legal, facilities) that remain with seller
4. **Revenue attribution** — How revenue is currently booked vs. how it will flow to the carved-out entity
5. **IP and contract audit** — Which patents, licenses, and customer contracts are cleanly assignable

### Signing to Close
1. **TSA negotiation** — Scope, duration (typically 12–24 months), pricing (cost + 5–15% markup), SLAs
2. **Day 1 readiness planning** — Minimum viable standalone capabilities needed at close
3. **Regulatory clearance** — Antitrust review (Hart-Scott-Rodino in US, EU merger control if applicable)
4. **Employee transfer planning** — WARN Act compliance, retention packages, cultural integration
5. **Customer communication plan** — Coordinated messaging to prevent churn during transition

### Post-Close Integration
1. **TSA execution** — Monitor SLA compliance, track costs, plan TSA exit milestones
2. **Standalone build-out** — Replace TSA services with internal or third-party capabilities
3. **Synergy capture** — Track cost synergies (headcount, facilities, vendor consolidation) and revenue synergies
4. **100-day plan** — Quick wins for customer retention, employee engagement, operational stability

## Stranded Cost Analysis Framework

| Cost Category | Typical Range (% of carved-out rev) | Key Questions |
|---|---|---|
| IT infrastructure | 3–5% | Shared ERP, CRM, data centers, networking |
| HR & payroll | 1–2% | HRIS, benefits administration, recruiting |
| Finance & accounting | 1–3% | Shared services center, audit, tax, treasury |
| Legal & compliance | 0.5–1% | Corporate legal, regulatory, IP management |
| Facilities | 1–3% | Shared offices, labs, manufacturing space |
| Executive overhead | 1–2% | C-suite allocation, board costs, corporate comms |
| **Total estimated** | **7–16%** | Rule of thumb: 10–12% of revenue for large carve-outs |

## TSA Best Practices

- **Duration**: 12–24 months maximum; avoid extensions beyond 24 months
- **Pricing**: Cost + 5–15% for standard services; market rate for specialized services
- **Exit triggers**: Define clear milestones and exit criteria per service
- **Penalties**: Include seller performance SLAs with financial remedies
- **Parallel build**: Begin standalone build immediately at close — don't wait for TSA to expire
- **Governance**: Joint steering committee with monthly reviews

## Accretion/Dilution Modeling Checklist

1. Acquirer standalone EPS (GAAP and non-GAAP)
2. Target standalone earnings (normalize for carve-out adjustments)
3. Purchase price and funding structure (debt/equity/cash mix)
4. Incremental interest expense on new debt
5. Shares issued (if equity component)
6. Amortization of intangible assets (customer relationships, IP, brand)
7. Synergies (phased: 30% Y1, 60% Y2, 90% Y3)
8. Integration and restructuring charges (one-time)
9. Tax impacts (purchase price allocation, NOL utilization)
10. Pro forma combined EPS vs. standalone — accretive or dilutive by year

## Technology Carve-Out Specific Risks (2025–2026 Trends)

Per KPMG (Feb 2026): 57% of corporates are pursuing carve-outs, driven by:
- AI and technology disruption accelerating divestiture decisions
- Portfolio simplification as companies focus on core capabilities
- Improved financing conditions making buyouts more attractive

Per Morrison Foerster (Jan 2026):
- Technology M&A increased 66% YoY to ~$1.08T in 2025
- Data infrastructure and cybersecurity driving deal flow
- Regulatory scrutiny elevated but navigable with early engagement
