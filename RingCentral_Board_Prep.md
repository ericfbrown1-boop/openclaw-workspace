# RingCentral Board Prep Briefing
Prepared for: Eric Brown  
Date: March 19, 2026  
Sources: RingCentral investor relations home page (ir.ringcentral.com), RingCentral Q4 & FY23 earnings release (Feb 20, 2024, corporate press site), RingCentral product/AI announcements

---

## 1. Executive Snapshot
- **Business**: AI-driven cloud communications, contact center, video, and hybrid event solutions serving 100+ countries. FY23 revenue $2.20B (+11% YoY) with 96% recurring subscription mix.
- **Scale & mix**: Total ARR $2.33B (+11%), enterprise ARR >$1.0B (+13%), mid-market & enterprise ARR $1.46B (+12%).
- **Profitability & cash**: Non-GAAP operating margin expanded to 19.1% in FY23 (up 670 bps). Adjusted EBITDA $503M (22.8% margin). Operating cash flow $400M (18.1% of revenue); adj. unlevered FCF $325M (14.8%).
- **Strategic pivot**: "AI-first, multi-product" roadmap anchored by RingSense™ AI platform, RingCX™ native contact center, and RingCentral Events (Hopin acquisition). Focus on durable, profitable growth.

## 2. Business & Market Context
- **Core platform (RingEX/MVP)**: Cloud UCaaS suite for voice, meetings, messaging. Differentiation in telephony depth, global footprint, and telco partnerships.
- **Adjacencies**:
  - **RingCX™**: Native AI-first contact center tightly integrated with MVP; disrupts legacy CCaaS pricing and simplifies deployment for mid-market.
  - **RingCentral Events**: Hybrid/virtual events platform (former Hopin) broadens engagement layer.
  - **RingSense™ AI**: Conversation intelligence framework that powers auto-summaries, follow-ups, CRM sync, and coaching across UC+CC products. Recent expansion includes open APIs and workflow builder.
- **Competitive landscape**: Microsoft Teams, Zoom, Cisco Webex on UCaaS; Genesys, NICE, Five9 on CCaaS. Differentiation depends on AI-enabled workflows, telco-grade reliability, and partner ecosystem (Avaya, Vodafone, etc.).
- **Customer mix**: Enterprise & mid-market ARR now ~63% of total, reflecting pivot away from lower-margin SMB. Board focus: ensure enterprise momentum offsets SMB churn while maintaining profitability.

### Trend takeaways
- ARR growth outpaces revenue, signaling improving net expansion.
- Mix shift to enterprise yields higher gross margin and retention.
- Operating leverage from cost discipline + AI automation is driving record FCF; board should probe sustainability of ~20% cash margins.

## 4. AI & Product Innovation
- **RingSense™ AI platform**: Conversation intelligence across calls, video, and contact center interactions; now exposes APIs for transcript, summarization, diarization, and workflow automation. Positioned as differentiator vs. commodity AI copilots.
- **RingSense for Sales / ACE**: Auto-logging to CRM, follow-up generation, live coaching. Opportunity to upsell existing UCaaS base with per-seat AI SKUs.
- **RingCX™ contact center**: Native, AI-first CCaaS built for simplicity vs. legacy Engage. Pricing/packaging designed for fast deployments; integrates with MVP for unified agent + knowledge workflows.
- **RingCentral Events**: Adds event analytics + AI-driven engagement, complementing RingSense insights.
- **AI monetization levers**:
  - Upsell modules (RingSense add-on, AI minutes)
  - Value-based packaging (RingCX bundles)
  - Efficiency (auto summaries reduce support costs; internal use to lower PS/S&M).
- **Data/privacy considerations**: Must maintain compliance for regulated verticals; highlight that AI operates on customer-owned data with enterprise-grade security.

## 5. Key Watch Areas
1. **AI differentiation vs. hyperscalers**: Microsoft/Zoom launching comparable copilots; need clarity on moats (telephony data, cross-channel insights, open APIs).
2. **Enterprise ARR durability**: >$1B milestone, but requires continued large-deal wins and low churn amid macro scrutiny.
3. **Margin sustainability**: Non-GAAP op margin now >20%; board should monitor reinvestment pace into AI & CCaaS to maintain growth while protecting FCF.
4. **Capital allocation**: Balance between debt reduction (convert repurchase), share buybacks, and potential tuck-in AI acquisitions.
5. **Platform convergence risks**: Integrating Events, RingCX, and RingSense without over-complicating product/UX.

## 6. Discussion Guide for Vlad Shmunis (AI-focused)
1. **AI monetization roadmap**: How do you plan to price and bundle RingSense capabilities across UCaaS, CCaaS, and Events so AI attach exceeds 50% of enterprise seats over the next 12–18 months?
2. **Competitive moat vs. Microsoft/Zoom**: What proprietary data assets or telco integrations will keep RingSense differentiated as hyperscalers roll out similar copilots embedded in Teams/Office?
3. **Contact center synergy**: How will RingCX + RingSense jointly accelerate enterprise wins, and what KPIs (ARR, NRR) should we watch to gauge traction in FY24?
4. **AI governance & customer trust**: What safeguards (data residency, SOC2, model explainability) are in place so regulated customers can confidently adopt AI features without compliance friction?
5. **Resource allocation**: Given record cash flow, how are you balancing further AI R&D (models, APIs, workflow builder) against debt reduction and buybacks to maintain strategic flexibility?
