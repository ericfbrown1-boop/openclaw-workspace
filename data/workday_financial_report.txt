and $8.83B in subscription revenue (+14.5% YoY), meeting guidance while expanding
non-GAAP operating margin by 370 bps to 29.6%. The company is executing a deliberate
AI pivot, positioning itself as "the enterprise AI platform for managing people, money,
and agents" — a message underscored by the Q4 acquisition of Sana and the March 2026
launch of "Sana from Workday." Stock trades at $124.21 (as of March 27, 2026), implying
~1.3x NTM revenue. With 41 sell-side analysts covering the name, sentiment is broadly
constructive on the AI opportunity but watchful of deceleration from 13% revenue growth.

4. AI STRATEGY — "SANA FROM WORKDAY" LAUNCH (March 2026)

This is the most important strategic development in the quarter and warrants
detailed treatment given its implications for competitive positioning.

What is Sana?
  Workday acquired Sana (AI startup) in Q4 FY26. On March 27, 2026, they launched
  "Sana from Workday" — described as "superintelligence for work."

Three-tier product structure:
  1. Sana for Workday — new conversational AI interface replacing traditional Workday UI
  2. Sana Self-Service Agent — 300+ skills for HR and finance self-service tasks
  3. Sana Enterprise — connects Workday with third-party apps:
     Gmail, Salesforce, SharePoint, Box, Confluence, Jira, Slack, Zoom, Notion, Miro, Linear

Positioning:
  "AI only works in the enterprise when it's connected to trusted, deterministic systems,
   and that hybrid architecture is exactly what Workday is building. Sana is what brings
   it all together." — Aneel Bhusri, CEO

Early Traction (customer quote):
  "Within 40 days, Sana became our default AI interface at work — we reached 90% adoption
   and retired 400 ChatGPT licenses." — Joona Honka, Head of AI & Analytics, Berner

Monetization:
  Available to ALL existing Workday customers via Workday Flex Credits (no new license
  required). This is an attach/retention play first, upsell later. Similar to Salesforce's
  initial Agentforce positioning — land wide, monetize on consumption.

Analyst implication:
  Sana could materially compress Workday's reliance on traditional subscription
  growth and accelerate consumption-based revenue. However, the "no extra cost"
  initial availability may weigh on near-term ASP expansion.

8. KEY RISKS & WATCH ITEMS

1. Growth deceleration: Revenue growth has slowed from ~20%+ (FY22-23) to ~13% (FY26).
   FY27 guidance implies further modest deceleration (12-13%). Bull case requires AI
   monetization to reverse this trend by FY28.

2. Sana monetization timing: Initial "free with Flex Credits" packaging is smart for
   adoption but delays revenue recognition. Watch FY27 for consumption-based pricing signals.

3. Restructuring charges: $303M in FY26 (vs $84M in FY25) — elevated. Should normalize
   in FY27, providing ~$200M+ tailwind to GAAP earnings.

4. Competition: ServiceNow, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM, Microsoft Dynamics are
   all investing heavily in AI agents. Sana's differentiation is the trusted Workday
   data layer — this is the key moat claim to monitor.

5. Macro exposure: Large enterprise HR/Finance software is resilient but not immune.
   Any slowdown in Fortune 500 hiring or IT spend could pressure net new ACV.
