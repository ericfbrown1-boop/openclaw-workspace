## SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Quarter Snapshot

Commvault (NASDAQ: CVLT) reported Q3 FY2026 results on January 27, 2026, delivering **accelerating growth** across key metrics. The company achieved its strongest customer acquisition quarter in company history and launched the Cloud Unity Platform in November 2025, positioning for continued momentum.

### Key Headline Numbers

| Metric | Q3 FY2026 Actual | YoY Growth | Sequential |
|--------|------------------|------------|------------|
| **Total Revenue** | $314M | +19% | +14% |
| **Subscription Revenue** | $206M | +30% | +19% |
| **Total ARR** | $1,085M | +22% (17% cc) | +4% |
| **Subscription ARR** | $941M | +28% (24% cc) | +5% |
| **SaaS ARR** | $364M | +40% | +8% |
| **SaaS NRR** | 121% | -400 bps | -400 bps |

| **Free Cash Flow** | $2M | -$28M | -$72M |
| **Rule of 40** | 41% | Achieved | ✓ |

### Overall Assessment: **POSITIVE WITH CAUTION FLAGS**

**Positives:**
- Revenue growth **accelerated** to 19% from 18% prior quarter
- Best new customer quarter **ever** for term software
- Second-best SaaS customer acquisition quarter in history
- Subscription ARR growth of 28% YoY (24% constant currency) remains strong
- Achieved Rule of 40 (41%) demonstrating healthy growth/profitability balance
- Cloud Unity Platform launched with strong market reception

**Concerns:**
- SaaS NRR declined 400 bps sequentially to 121% (from 125%)
- Free cash flow of only $2M severely impacted by timing issues
- Net new ARR of $42M missed expectations ($45M implied)
- Initiated cost optimization (restructuring) program at quarter-end
- Q4 guidance implies **deceleration** to 11% total revenue growth
- Term deal durations elongated, creating ARR headwinds

### 2.1 Total Revenue Performance

**Q3 FY2026: $314 Million (+19% YoY)**

Commvault delivered **accelerating revenue growth**, up from 18% in Q2 and 26% in Q1. The 19% growth represents strong execution but includes easier prior-year comparisons.

#### Revenue Trend Analysis (Last 5 Quarters)

| Quarter | Revenue | YoY Growth | QoQ Growth | Acceleration/(Decel) |
|---------|---------|------------|------------|----------------------|
| Q3'25 | $263M | +21% | +13% | — |

- However, Q4 guidance of $305-308M implies only 11% growth at midpoint
- **Red Flag:** Significant Q4 deceleration suggests deal timing/linearity issues

### 2.2 Revenue Mix Breakdown

#### Subscription Revenue: $206M (+30% YoY)

Subscription revenue continues to be the **growth engine**, now representing **66% of total revenue** (up from 60% in Q3'25).

  - Average deal duration increased (elongated multiyear deals)
  - Deals >$100K grew 25% YoY
  - Million-dollar deal volume increased

- **SaaS Revenue:** $87M (+44% YoY)
  - Second-highest customer acquisition quarter
  - 700 new subscription customers added
  - Now over 14,000 subscription customers (9,000+ SaaS)

## SECTION 5: RED FLAGS & CONCERNS

### 5.1 Critical Red Flags

#### **1. SaaS Net Dollar Retention Declined to 121% (from 125%)**

**Details:**
- **Q2'26 NRR:** 125%
- **Q3'26 NRR:** 121%
- **Change:** -400 basis points sequentially

**Management Explanation:**
1. **Larger customer base effect:** Now 9,000+ SaaS customers (growing base = harder to maintain high NRR%)
2. **New customer mix:** Strong Q3 new customer adds don't show up in NRR yet (trailing metric)
3. **Product mix shift:** "Modest mix shift in some of our product capabilities among certain early adopter customers"

**Why This Matters:**
- NRR is **critical metric** for SaaS companies
- 121% is still "best-in-class" per management
- But declining trend (from prior quarters likely >125%) is concerning
- Suggests either:
  - Slower expansion in existing base
  - Higher churn/downgrades
  - Product transitions causing contraction

**Cohesity Competitive Context:**
- Cohesity's NRR metrics would be a useful comparison
- If Commvault NRR continues declining while competitor NRR stable/improving, indicates market share loss
- Watch for mention of **Rubrik** (recently IPO'd), **Cohesity**, **Veeam** competitive dynamics

#### **2. Free Cash Flow Collapsed to $2M (from $74M in Q2)**

**Q3'26 Free Cash Flow: $2 Million**

**Comparison:**

| Quarter | Operating Cash Flow | CapEx | Free Cash Flow | FCF Margin |
|---------|---------------------|-------|----------------|------------|
| Q3'25 | $30M | $0.3M | $30M | 11% |
| Q2'26 | $77M | $3M | $74M | 27% |
| **Q3'26** | **$4M** | **$2M** | **$2M** | **0.6%** |

**Management Explanation:**
1. **Deal Linearity:** Over 60% of deals closed in last few weeks of quarter
2. **Collections Timing:** Deals closed late → receivables outstanding → not collected
3. **Extra Payroll Cycle:** US and Canada had extra payroll cycle in Q3 (timing item)
4. **Expected to Normalize:** Q4 FCF expected ~$112M (implies ~$110M improvement)

**Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) Analysis:**

| Quarter | AR Balance | Revenue (quarterly) | Implied DSO |
|---------|------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Q2'26 | $235M | $276M | ~77 days |
| Q3'26 | $362M | $314M | ~104 days |
| **Change** | +$127M | +$38M | **+27 days** |

**Why This Matters:**
- **Significant DSO deterioration** (77 → 104 days)
- Suggests deals closed late in quarter with extended payment terms
- Could indicate:
  - Competitive pressure requiring longer terms
  - Customers delaying payment
  - End-of-quarter deal stuffing
- **Pattern to Watch:** If Q4 FCF doesn't recover to ~$110M, major red flag

**Full Year FCF Guidance Lowered:**
- Previous: $225-230M
- Current: $215-220M
- Reduction: ~$10M (includes $12-15M restructuring, so operational FCF actually up slightly)

#### **3. Net New ARR of $42M Missed Expectations**

**Q3'26 Net New ARR: $42 Million (constant currency: $39M)**

**Management guided for mid-$40Ms in Q3**
- Last quarter call: "we expect net new ARR in the mid-forties"
- Actual: $39M constant currency
- **Miss of ~$6M or 13%**

**Breakdown:**
- **SaaS Net New ARR:** $27M (70% of total)
- **Term Software Net New ARR:** $18M (calculated, 43% of total)
- **Other ARR:** Negative (support decline)

**Why Lower Than Expected:**

**Management Explanation:**
1. **SaaS ASP Effect:** 70% of net new ARR was SaaS, which lands at 2-3x lower ASP than software
   - SaaS land ASP: ~$40K
   - Software land ASP: ~$100K+
   - More SaaS wins = lower initial ARR despite higher long-term value

2. **Term Duration Elongation:** Large new software customers signed longer-duration deals
   - Longer durations → lower annualized ARR calculation
   - Example: $300K 3-year deal = $100K ARR vs $150K 1-year deal = $150K ARR
   - Volume of million-dollar+ deals increased

**Analyst Concern (from Q&A):**
- Analyst: "Net new ARR for total was $39M. I believe on the last earnings call you guys had talked about mid-forties. So I'm curious, like, what drove that?"
- Management confirmed term duration and SaaS mix were primary drivers

**Why This Matters:**
- **ARR is forward-looking metric** - miss suggests growth deceleration
- **Deal structure shifting** - longer durations could indicate competitive pressure
- **SaaS ASP dynamics** - need to monitor if SaaS expansion can offset lower land ASP
- **Pattern:** If net new ARR continues missing in Q4, major concern for FY'27 growth

#### **4. Cost Optimization / Restructuring Program Initiated**

**Timing:** End of Q3 (late December 2025 / early January 2026)

**Costs:**
- **One-time restructuring costs:** $12-15M expected in Q4
- **Q3 restructuring charge:** $12M already recorded
- **Total program:** ~$25-28M

**Details:**
- **Company-wide cost optimization** program
- Includes:
  - Workforce reductions (voluntary retirement program "well received")
  - Operating expense realignment
  - NOT focused on R&D (per management)
- Goal: "Align cost structure to evolving needs of business"

**Why This Matters:**
- **Initiated at end of Q3** suggests urgency / unexpected pressure
- **Q4 guidance deceleration** may have triggered cost actions
- **FCF pressure** likely contributed to decision
- **Red flag if:** Restructuring leads to sales force disruption or product delays
- **Positive if:** Successfully rebalances spend toward high-growth areas

**Management Spin:**
- "Strengthens where we want to go, not weakens"
- "Really about strengthening where we think the opportunity lies"
- "Aligning the business to priorities for next fiscal year"

**Historical Context:**

#### **Gross Retention vs Net Retention**

**Note:** Commvault does NOT disclose **gross retention rate** (GRR)
